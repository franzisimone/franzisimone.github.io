Bio

I am a researcher focused on the political economy of trade and finance. Currently, I am a doctoral student in the School of Public & International Affairs and I work as a research assistant at the Global Forum on Resilience at Virginia Tech. My current research examines the political economy of trade and trade agreements. I work also on technological innovations and their impacts to political institutions. In particular, I am interested in the regulatory aspect of blockchain technology. I also write and lecture about the co-evolution of capitalism and democracy, processes of democratization, and the Swiss political system, Swiss education, and Swiss-US relations. In my blog, I comment and reflect on the United States democratic institutions from a Swiss perspective.

In the news: Interview "Gli Espatriati", Interview to the Swiss-Italian Public Radio Station (RSI), 1-12-2017.
